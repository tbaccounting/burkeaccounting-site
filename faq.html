Frequently Asked Questions

Plain-English answers about how BurkeAccounting works.

What does it mean that BurkeAccounting is a non-CPA tax preparer?

It means BurkeAccounting provides tax preparation and basic bookkeeping services but is not a Certified Public Accountant (CPA) firm. BurkeAccounting does not issue audited, reviewed, or compiled financial statements and does not represent clients in IRS court proceedings. For situations that legally require a CPA, you will be referred to a licensed professional.

How does the pricing cap work?

Your fee starts with a base price (for example, $20 for simple W-2 returns). Then a small percentage of your tax outcome (refund or amount owed) is added, plus any complexity charges if your return is more involved. Regardless, the total fee for a single tax return will never exceed $270.

Can BurkeAccounting help if I have self-employment income?

Yes. If you have 1099 income, online gig income, or a small business, BurkeAccounting can prepare Schedule C and related forms for many typical self-employment situations. Very complex businesses or those requiring advanced entity tax planning may be referred to a CPA or tax attorney.

How do I upload my documents?

You can use your own Google Drive or Dropbox folder and share a secure link, or use another mutually agreed-upon secure method. BurkeAccounting will give you a simple checklist of what to upload (W-2s, 1099s, prior-year return, etc.).

Can BurkeAccounting give me legal or investment advice?

No. BurkeAccounting does not provide legal advice, investment recommendations, or representation in legal proceedings. Any general information provided is for educational purposes only and should not be relied upon as legal or financial advice.
